Trade Me Overview

How Trade Me Came to Be

In 1999, Sam Morgan saw an opportunity to create New Zealand’s first online marketplace after experiencing frustration with outdated newspaper classifieds. With a small team, Trade Me launched as a site for buying, selling, and auctioning goods, with real-time listings that quickly became popular.

Growth of Trade Me

Initially a small operation, Trade Me grew rapidly. Through grassroots promotion and word of mouth, membership hit 100,000 users by 2003. It soon became the go-to marketplace in New Zealand, earning recognition as the fastest-growing company in 2004.

Key Milestones

  • 2001: Launch of the FindSomeone dating service.
  • 2003: Introduction of Trade Me Motors.
  • 2004: Launch of Trade Me Property for real estate listings.
  • 2006: Acquisition by Fairfax Media for NZ$700 million.
  • 2019: Acquired by Apax Partners and delisted from the NZX and ASX.

Trade Me Review: A Marketplace Leader?

Trade Me has become synonymous with online shopping in New Zealand, but how does it compare to other platforms like Facebook Marketplace? Below, we break down the platform’s strengths and areas for improvement.

Pros and Cons of Trade Me

Pros

  • Extensive range of categories (vehicles, property, jobs, general goods).
  • Built-in buyer and seller protections, adding security to transactions.
  • Instant payment options through Ping and Afterpay.
  • Large, active user base with millions of listings at any time.
  • Comprehensive search and filter tools for better browsing.

Cons

  • Success fees can be high for expensive items.
  • Competition from free platforms like Facebook Marketplace.
  • No free shipping options built into the platform; dependent on sellers.

Our Verdict on Trade Me

Trade Me remains the most comprehensive and secure online marketplace in New Zealand, offering categories and services beyond what competitors provide. While success fees may deter some sellers, the platform's reliability, security, and range of listings make it a solid choice for buyers and sellers alike.
