Trade Me Review

How Trade Me Came to Be

Back in 1999, the idea of buying from strangers online seemed unusual for many New Zealanders, but **Sam Morgan** saw an opportunity. Frustrated by the outdated newspaper classifieds while searching for a heater, he realized the potential for a digital marketplace. Combining his IT expertise with this idea, Sam developed the first version of Trade Me in just six weeks.

At the time, the concept of live online auctions was rare in New Zealand, but it was quickly implemented on the new platform. Trade Me launched with four employees and a small group of 20 members, mostly friends and family.

Growth of Trade Me

From its humble beginnings, Trade Me quickly grew. Sam tirelessly promoted the platform by driving around Wellington in a bright green Holden with Trade Me branding and distributing flyers. By the end of its first few months, the site reached 1,000 users, and by 2003, the number had skyrocketed to 100,000 members.

In 2004, Trade Me was recognized as New Zealand’s fastest-growing company at the Deloitte/Unlimited Fast 50 Awards. By 2006, the platform had over a million registered members and had become the most visited website in New Zealand, averaging over two million visitors per month.

Expansion and Key Milestones

As Trade Me expanded, new services were introduced to meet the growing demands of users:

  • 2001: FindSomeone, a dating service, was launched.
  • 2003: Trade Me Motors opened for buying and selling vehicles.
  • 2004: Trade Me Property was introduced for real estate listings.
  • 2006: Trade Me Jobs launched, providing a platform for employment opportunities.

The Sale and Transition

In 2006, Sam Morgan sold Trade Me to Fairfax Media for NZ$700 million, though he continued as CEO until 2008, when he passed the leadership to Jon McDonald. Trade Me’s influence continued to grow, leading to its public listing on both the NZX and ASX in 2011 under the ticker "TME".

In 2019, Apax Partners acquired the company for NZ$2.56 billion, delisting it from the stock market. Leadership changed again as Anders Skoe took over as CEO, guiding the company into its latest chapter.

Trade Me Today

Today, Trade Me remains New Zealand’s largest online marketplace. With millions of listings, from cars and property to everyday goods, it has become the go-to platform for Kiwis looking to buy and sell with ease. The introduction of modern features like Ping (an instant payment system) and Afterpay has kept the platform relevant and user-friendly.
